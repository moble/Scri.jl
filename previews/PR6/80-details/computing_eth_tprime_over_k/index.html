Computing $√∞t'/k$

The combination $√∞t'/k$ appears in the BMS transformation of the Weyl components. Thus, efficiently computing it is a key part of the BMS pipeline.

We have the coordinate transformation

\[\begin{gather} u' = k(u + ѕГбµЕ ќ±), \\ \frac{1}{k} = ќ≥(1 - ѕГвБ±vвГЧвЛЕnћВ), \end{gather}\]

where $ѕГбµЕ$ accounts for differences in the definition of the supertranslation parameter $ќ±$, and $ѕГвБ±$ accounts for whether we're dealing with $вДРвБЇ$ ($ѕГвБ± = +1$) or $вДРвБї$ ($ѕГвБ± = -1$).

Note that $1/k$ has a simple form вАФ and specifically, it decomposes into a pure $вДУ = 0$ plus a pure $вДУ = 1$ function on the sphere. This allows us to compute $√∞(1/k)$ in closed form, without needing to evaluate the synthesis sum at all. The $√∞$ operator is a derivation, meaning that it obeys the Leibniz rule, so as usual we have $√∞(k) = -√∞(1/k) k^2$.

\[\begin{align} \frac{√∞t'}{k} &= -√∞(1/k) k (u + ѕГбµЕ ќ±) + ѕГбµЕ √∞ќ± \\ &= \frac{ѕГвБ± √∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ)}{1 - ѕГвБ±vвГЧвЛЕnћВ} (u + ѕГбµЕ ќ±) + ѕГбµЕ √∞ќ±. \end{align}\]

Since $ќ±$ and $√∞ќ±$ may contain вАФ in principle вАФ arbitrarily high $вДУ$ modes, it will be best to compute them via SphericalFunctions.jl. However, the $vвГЧвЛЕnћВ$ and $√∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ)$ terms are simple enough that it will be more efficient to compute them directly in closed form, using rotor components. That is the objective of what follows.

Computing $√∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ)$

So we have simplified the problem to computing $√∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ)$. The product $vвГЧвЛЕnћВ$ is an $s=0$, $вДУ = 1$ function. The $√∞$ operator has a very simple action in mode space, so one approach is to just evaluate the mode weights of $vвГЧвЛЕnћВ$ and apply the known mode-space formula for $√∞$.

Given the standard spherical-harmonic formulas

\[\begin{aligned} Y_{1,0}(ќЄ, ѕЖ) &= \sqrt{\frac{3}{4ѕА}}\,\cos ќЄ, \\ Y_{1,\pm 1}(ќЄ, ѕЖ) &= \mp\sqrt{\frac{3}{8ѕА}}\,\sin ќЄ\,e^{\pm iѕЖ}, \end{aligned}\]

we have

\[\begin{aligned} \cos ќЄ &= \sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\,Y_{1,0}, \\ \sin ќЄ \cos ѕЖ &= -\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}}\,\left(Y_{1,1} - Y_{1,-1}\right), \\ \sin ќЄ \sin ѕЖ &= \frac{i}{2}\sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}}\,\left(Y_{1,1} + Y_{1,-1}\right). \end{aligned}\]

We can re-express the dot product as

\[\begin{aligned} vвГЧвЛЕnћВ &= v_x\sin ќЄ\cos ѕЖ + v_y\sin ќЄ\sin ѕЖ + v_z\cos ќЄ \\ &= -v_x\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}}\,\left(Y_{1,1} - Y_{1,-1}\right) + v_y\frac{i}{2}\sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}}\,\left(Y_{1,1} + Y_{1,-1}\right) + v_z \sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}} Y_{1,0} \\ \end{aligned}\]

Now, we also know that the $√∞$ operator acts on spin-weighted spherical harmonics by

\[√∞ {}_{s}Y_{вДУ,m} = \sqrt{(вДУ-s)(вДУ+s+1)}\, {}_{s+1}Y_{вДУ,m}.\]

Of course, with $s=0$ and $вДУ=1$, the factor is just $\sqrt{2}$, so we have

\[\begin{aligned} √∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ) &= -v_x\sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\,\left({}_{1}Y_{1,1} - {}_{1}Y_{1,-1}\right) + v_y i\sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\,\left({}_{1}Y_{1,1} + {}_{1}Y_{1,-1}\right) + v_z \sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}} {}_{1}Y_{1,0} \\ &= (-v_x+ v_y i)\sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\,{}_{1}Y_{1,1} + (v_x+ v_y i)\sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\, {}_{1}Y_{1,-1} + v_z \sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}} {}_{1}Y_{1,0} \end{aligned}\]

Another approach is to use the original form of $√∞$ in terms of partial derivatives with respect to spherical coordinates. For $s=0$, it is just

\[√∞ f = -\left(\partial_ќЄ f + \frac{i}{\sin ќЄ}\,\partial_ѕЖ f\right).\]

Applying this to the explicit formula for $vвГЧвЛЕnћВ$ given above, we have

\[\begin{aligned} √∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ) &= -v_x\cos ќЄ\cos ѕЖ - v_y\cos ќЄ\sin ѕЖ + v_z\sin ќЄ - \frac{i}{\sin ќЄ} \left(-v_x\sin ќЄ\sin ѕЖ + v_y\sin ќЄ\cos ѕЖ\right) \\ &= -v_x(\cos ќЄ\cos ѕЖ - i\sin ѕЖ) - v_y(\cos ќЄ\sin ѕЖ + i\cos ѕЖ) + v_z\sin ќЄ \end{aligned}\]

We have a pair of expressions for Wikipedia:

\[\begin{aligned} {}_1 Y_{10}(\theta,\phi) &= \sqrt{\frac{3}{8\pi}}\,\sin\theta, \\ {}_1 Y_{1\pm 1}(\theta,\phi) &= -\sqrt{\frac{3}{16\pi}}(1 \mp \cos\theta)\,e^{\pm i\phi}. \end{aligned}\]

Plugging these in, we can verify that the two approaches give the same result.

However, it will be better to compute this directly in terms of the rotor components, which is more efficient and numerically well-behaved (no need to evaluate any trigonometric functions, and no spurious poles at the poles). This is the objective of the next section.

SWSHs and quaternions

The SWSH is related to Wigner's $D$ matrix by

\[{}_{s}Y_{\ell,m}(R) = (-1)^{s} \sqrt{ \frac{2\ell + 1} {4\pi}} \mathfrak{D}^{(\ell)}_{m, -s} (R),\]

and

\[ \mathfrak{D}^{(\ell)}_{m',m}(R) = \sum_{\rho} \binom{\ell+m'} {\rho}\, \binom{\ell-m'} {\ell-\rho-m}\, (-1)^{\rho}\, R_s^{\ell+m'-\rho}\, \bar{R}_s^{\ell-\rho-m}\, R_a^{\rho-m'+m}\, \bar{R}_a^{\rho}\, \sqrt{ \frac{ (\ell+m)!\, (\ell-m)! } { (\ell+m')!\, (\ell-m')! } },\]

where $R_s = R_1 + i R_z$ and $R_a = R_y + i R_x$. This gives a direct way to evaluate the SWSHs in terms of the rotor components, without needing to compute any trigonometric functions.

\[\begin{gather} R = \cos(ќЄ/2) \cos(ѕХ/2) - рЭРҐ \sin(ќЄ/2) \sin(ѕХ/2) + рЭР£ \sin(ќЄ/2) \cos(ѕХ/2) + рЭР§ \cos(ќЄ/2) \sin(ѕХ/2), \\ R_s = R_1 + i R_z = \cos(ќЄ/2) \cos(ѕХ/2) + i \cos(ќЄ/2) \sin(ѕХ/2) = \cos(ќЄ/2) e^{iѕХ/2}, \\ R_a = R_y + i R_x = \sin(ќЄ/2) \cos(ѕХ/2) + i (-\sin(ќЄ/2) \sin(ѕХ/2)) = \sin(ќЄ/2) e^{-iѕХ/2}. \end{gather}\]

\[\begin{aligned} {}_{1}Y_{1,m}(R) &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {4\pi}} \mathfrak{D}^{(1)}_{m, -1} (R) \\ &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {4\pi}} \sum_{\rho} \binom{1+m} {\rho}\, \binom{1-m} {2-\rho}\, (-1)^{\rho}\, R_s^{1+m-\rho}\, \bar{R}_s^{2-\rho}\, R_a^{\rho-m-1}\, \bar{R}_a^{\rho}\, \sqrt{ \frac{ 2 } { (1+m)!\, (1-m)! } } \end{aligned}\]

For $m=0$, we must have $\rho=1$:

\[\begin{aligned} {}_{1}Y_{1,0}(R) &= \sqrt{\frac{3} {2\pi}} \bar{R}_s\, \bar{R}_a \\ &= \sqrt{\frac{3} {2\pi}} \sin(ќЄ/2) \cos(ќЄ/2) \\ &= \sqrt{\frac{3} {8\pi}} \sin(ќЄ) \end{aligned}\]

For $m=1$ we have $\rho=2$:

\[\begin{aligned} {}_{1}Y_{1,1}(R) &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {4\pi}} \bar{R}_a^{2} \\ &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {4\pi}} \sin^2(ќЄ/2) e^{iѕХ} \\ &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {16\pi}} (1 - \cos ќЄ) e^{iѕХ} \end{aligned}\]

For $m=-1$, we have $\rho=0$:

\[\begin{aligned} {}_{1}Y_{1,-1}(R) &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {4\pi}} \bar{R}_s^{2} \\ &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {4\pi}} \cos^2(ќЄ/2) e^{-iѕХ} \\ &= -\sqrt{\frac{3} {16\pi}} (1 + \cos ќЄ) e^{-iѕХ} \end{aligned}\]

We have derived the same expressions in terms of spherical coordinates as are found on Wikipedia, but we have also found their simple expressions in terms of the rotor components, which is what we will actually use. We now express the full result

Expressing $√∞t'/k$ with rotor components

A significant simplification will come from the fact that the rotation of v by a rotor R is expressed as R*v*conj(R) = R(v) with this result:

function (R::Rotor)(v::QuatVec)
    quatvec(SA[
        false,
        ((R[1]^2 + R[2]^2 - R[3]^2 - R[4]^2)*v[2]
            + (R[1]*R[3] + R[2]*R[4])*2v[4] + (R[2]*R[3] - R[1]*R[4])*2v[3]),
        ((R[1]^2 - R[2]^2 + R[3]^2 - R[4]^2)*v[3]
            + (R[2]*R[3] + R[1]*R[4])*2v[2] + (R[3]*R[4] - R[1]*R[2])*2v[4]),
        ((R[1]^2 + R[4]^2 - R[2]^2 - R[3]^2)*v[4]
            + (R[1]*R[2] + R[3]*R[4])*2v[3] + (R[2]*R[4] - R[1]*R[3])*2v[2])
    ])
end

Now, we expand the SWHSs in rotor components and find

\[\begin{aligned} √∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ) &= (-v_x + v_y i)\sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\,{}_{1}Y_{1,1} + (v_x + v_y i)\sqrt{\frac{4ѕА}{3}}\, {}_{1}Y_{1,-1} + v_z \sqrt{\frac{8ѕА}{3}} {}_{1}Y_{1,0} \\ &= (v_x - v_y i) \bar{R}_a^{2} - (v_x + v_y i) \bar{R}_s^{2} + 2v_z \bar{R}_s\, \bar{R}_a \\ &= (v_x - v_y i) (R_y - i R_x)^{2} - (v_x + v_y i) (R_1 - i R_z)^{2} + 2v_z (R_1 - i R_z)\, (R_y - i R_x) \\ &= (v_x - v_y i) (R_y^2 - R_x^2 - 2i R_x R_y) \\&\quad - (v_x + v_y i) (R_1^2 - R_z^2 - 2i R_1 R_z) \\&\quad + 2v_z (R_1 R_y - i R_y R_z - i R_1 R_x - R_x R_z) \\ &= v_x (- R_1^2 - R_x^2 + R_y^2 + R_z^2 + 2i (R_1 R_z - R_x R_y)) \\&\quad - i v_y (R_1^2 - R_x^2 + R_y^2 - R_z^2 - 2i (R_x R_y + R_1 R_z)) \\&\quad + 2v_z (R_1 R_y - i R_y R_z - i R_1 R_x - R_x R_z) \\ &= -\left( \tilde{R} \vec{v} R \right) \cdot \left( \hat{x} + i \hat{y} \right). \end{aligned}\]

Similarly, we have

\[\begin{aligned} vвГЧвЛЕnћВ &= (R_1^2 + R_z^2 - R_x^2 - R_y^2)v_z + 2(-R_1 R_x + R_y R_z)v_y + 2(R_x R_z + R_1 R_y)v_x \\ &= \left( \tilde{R} \vec{v} R \right) \cdot \hat{z} \end{aligned}\]

These are very easy to calculate. If we define $ќЫ = RћГvвГЧR$ then we have

\[\begin{align} \frac{√∞t'}{k} &= \frac{ѕГвБ± √∞(vвГЧвЛЕnћВ)}{1 - ѕГвБ±vвГЧвЛЕnћВ} (u + ѕГбµЕ ќ±) + ѕГбµЕ √∞ќ± \\ &= \frac{-ѕГвБ± (ќЫ_x + i ќЫ_y)}{1 - ѕГвБ± ќЫ_z} (u + ѕГбµЕ ќ±) + ѕГбµЕ √∞ќ±. \end{align}\]

The term proportional to $u$ will vary between time steps, so we factor out a term constant in time and one proportional to time:

\[\frac{√∞t'}{k} = \left(\frac{√∞t'}{k}\right)_0 + \left(\frac{√∞t'}{k}\right)_1 u,\]

where

\[\begin{aligned} \left(\frac{√∞t'}{k}\right)_0 &= ѕГбµЕ \left( \frac{ќЫ_x + i ќЫ_y}{ќЫ_z - ѕГвБ±} ќ± + √∞ќ± \right), \\ \left(\frac{√∞t'}{k}\right)_1 &= \frac{ќЫ_x + i ќЫ_y}{ќЫ_z - ѕГвБ±}. \end{aligned}\]
